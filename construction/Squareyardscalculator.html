How to Calculate Square Yards

The math takes two measurements and one division. Here is the formula:

The Formula

Square Yards = (Length ft × Width ft) ÷ 9

One square yard = 9 square feet. That is a 3 ft × 3 ft square. Divide any square footage by 9 and you have your square yards.

Here is a step-by-step example using a 12 ft × 15 ft bedroom:

  1. Measure your room: 12 ft long, 15 ft wide.
  2. Multiply: 12 × 15 = 180 square feet.
  3. Divide by 9: 180 ÷ 9 = 20 square yards.
  4. Add 10% waste: 20 × 1.10 = 22 square yards to order.

Square Yards Calculator for Carpet and Flooring

Carpet dealers quote in square yards — not square feet. Before you call Shaw Floors or Mohawk for a price, you need your number in square yards. The waste percentage you add depends on your project type. A straight-lay carpet in a square bedroom needs about 10% extra. A diagonal tile layout needs 15%. A pattern-match carpet from Armstrong Flooring or USFloors can need 20% because the pattern repeat forces extra cuts on every row.

Linear Yards vs Square Yards — What Is the Difference?

Here is the thing — this is the question that trips up most carpet buyers at the store. A linear yard and a square yard are not the same.

A carpet roll is 12 feet wide. When a dealer quotes "20 linear yards," they mean 20 yards of length along that 12-ft-wide roll. That equals 12 ft × 60 ft = 720 square feet = 80 square yards. A linear yard measures only length along the roll. A square yard measures actual area. The conversion: 1 linear yard of 12-ft carpet = 4 square yards.

Before placing any carpet order, ask your supplier: "Is this quote in linear yards or square yards?" Armstrong Flooring and USFloors both quote differently depending on the region and retailer.

Square Yards for Landscaping — Sod, Turf, and Gravel

Sod is sold by the square yard. So is TifTuf turf and most Scotts-branded sod products at major suppliers. The formula is the same — measure your backyard or garden area in feet, multiply length × width for square footage, then divide by 9.

A standard sod pallet covers about 450–500 sq ft — that is 50–56 square yards. A 1,000 sq ft lawn needs about 111 sq yd, or roughly 2 pallets. Add 5–8% for edge trimming around garden beds and driveways. For gravel and mulch, most bags list coverage in square feet — divide by 9 to convert to square yards. For large concrete pours, concrete is ordered in cubic yards — use our cubic yard calculator for that step.

Frequently Asked Questions About the Square Yards Calculator

How do I calculate square yards?
Multiply your room's length by width in feet. Then divide by 9. A 12 ft × 15 ft room = 180 sq ft ÷ 9 = 20 square yards. Add 10% waste to get your final order. The square yards calculator at the top handles this in seconds.

How many square feet are in a square yard?
There are exactly 9 square feet in one square yard. A yard is 3 feet, so a square yard is 3 ft × 3 ft = 9 sq ft. Divide any square footage by 9 to convert to square yards. Multiply square yards by 9 to reverse the conversion.

How many square yards is a 12×12 room?
A 12 × 12 ft room is 144 square feet. Divide by 9 to get exactly 16 square yards. For carpet, add 10% for waste — order about 17.6 square yards, rounded up to 18 when placing your order.

How many square yards is a 10×10 room?
A 10 × 10 ft room is 100 square feet. Divide by 9 to get approximately 11.1 square yards. When ordering carpet or flooring, round up to 12 square yards to account for cuts and waste.

How do I calculate square yards for an L-shaped room?
Split the L-shape into two separate rectangles. Measure each section in feet and multiply for the area. Add both areas together for total square footage. Divide by 9 for square yards. Add 15% waste for the extra seams an irregular room creates.

What is the difference between square yards and linear yards?
A square yard measures area — 3 ft × 3 ft = 9 sq ft. A linear yard measures length only. One linear yard of 12-ft-wide carpet = 12 ft × 3 ft = 36 sq ft = 4 square yards. Always ask your supplier whether their quote is in linear or square yards before ordering.

How do I measure square yards for sod or turf?
Measure your lawn's length and width in feet. Multiply for square footage and divide by 9 for square yards. A standard sod pallet covers 450–500 sq ft — about 50–56 sq yd. Add 5–8% for edge trimming. Enter your lawn dimensions in the square yards calculator above for an instant result.

How many square yards in an acre?
One acre equals exactly 4,840 square yards. That is 43,560 sq ft ÷ 9 = 4,840 sq yd. This conversion is used in large-scale landscaping and agricultural projects when estimating sod, seed, or fertiliser coverage per acre.

How do I convert square meters to square yards?
Multiply your square meter value by 1.196 to get square yards. For example, 20 sq m × 1.196 = 23.9 square yards. To reverse, multiply square yards by 0.836. This is most useful for UK, Canadian, and Australian material orders.

How much does a square yard of carpet cost?
Budget carpet runs $3–$8 per square yard. Mid-grade runs $10–$20 per square yard. Premium or wool carpet runs $25–$50+. A flooring installer adds $3–$8 per square yard for labor. These are 2024–2025 US prices — get a local quote for current regional rates.
