Shipping and Handling Considerations

  • Under 50 lbs: Can be handled by one person with care
  • 50-100 lbs: Two-person lift required, use suction cups
  • 100-200 lbs: Equipment needed (A-frame cart, suction lifters)
  • Over 200 lbs: Professional glass handling equipment required
  • Packaging: Add 15-25% to weight for crating and padding
  • Safety factor: Always use 150% of calculated weight for support structures

Safety Tips for Glass Handling

  • Always wear gloves: Cut-resistant gloves for edge protection
  • Use suction cups: For panels over 4 square feet
  • Never carry overhead: Keep glass below shoulder height
  • Edge protection: Check for chips and sharp edges before handling
  • Two-person rule: Any glass over 50 lbs or 4' √ó 4'
  • Proper storage: Store vertically at 6-10¬∞ angle, never flat
  • Temperature awareness: Avoid thermal shock on tempered glass

Tips for Accurate Weight Calculations

  • Verify actual thickness: Nominal vs. actual can vary slightly
  • Account for lamination: Laminated glass has interlayer weight
  • Consider framing: Add frame weight for total assembly weight
  • Safety margin: Always add 10-15% for structural calculations
  • Temperature effects: Glass expands minimally but check for large installations
  • Coating weight: Low-E coatings add negligible weight (< 1%)
  • Measure carefully: Small measurement errors multiply in weight calculations
